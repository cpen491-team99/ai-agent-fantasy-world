# Hail, Traveler!

Welcome to **Honeyveil**. This is a kingdom comprising of expansive mountain ranges, vast river systems, and lush wetlands. It is inhabited by anthropomorphic animals and magical creatures who are entirely unaware that their home and their very lives are a magical illusion. You are here to witness a story led by **Clover** (the hyper-energetic Wizard Fox), **Queen Seralith** (the elusive swan queen), **The Brave Little Mouse** (a legend with a secret), **Bramble Bunny** (the empathetic heart of the woods), and many more mythical creatures residing in the magical realm. 

## Chapter 1: The Fraying Stitches

Honeyveil is a masterpiece of artifice woven by Queen Seralith from thread spun from the stars. To maintain the illusion, she enchanted a needle to sew the veil’s seams automatically, guarded by a society of beetles who speak their own untranslatable language. However, the beetles lost track of the tiny needle five years ago; terrified of the Queen’s dark side, they have been lying to her ever since, claiming everything is fine.

Because the needle is missing, Honeyveil has begun to fray at the edges. Golden threads now snag on leaves, rocks, and even thin air—a phenomenon noticed by only a few observant residents. Clover, who is actually an untrained artificer, collects these threads under the title of "Wizard Fox," believing them to be the source of his magic. He spends his days experimenting with these fibers, often enlisting Bramble Bunny to help him bake "magic food" like levitating pies or truth-telling jams in her chestnut tree cottage.

While the self-absorbed Queen gazes at her reflection in the palace lagoons, the Brave Little Mouse stalks the shadows of Redberry Woods. Though he projects a mask of fearless heroism, he uses his tiny stature to eavesdrop on the beetles and toads, gathering rumors of "open seams" that lead out into the unknown world of Larkspur. As the threads of their reality continue to come loose, these unlikely neighbors are destined to realize that their perfect world is held together by a single, lost needle.
